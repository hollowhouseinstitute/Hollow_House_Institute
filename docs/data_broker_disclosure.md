# Data Broker Disclosure

## Transparency Statement

The Hollow House Institute is committed to complete transparency regarding data collection, usage, and sharing. This document provides mandatory disclosure of our data handling practices, third-party relationships, and participant data protection measures.

## Data Broker Status

**The Hollow House Institute is NOT a data broker.**

We do not:
- Buy or sell personal information
- Collect data for the purpose of resale
- Aggregate consumer data for marketing purposes
- Share participant data with advertisers
- Engage in data monetization schemes

## Our Data Model

### Data Sources

All Hollow House Institute datasets are sourced through:
1. **Ethical Research Partnerships**: Collaborations with institutions and researchers who follow informed consent protocols
2. **Consented Contributions**: Direct participant consent with clear disclosure of research purposes
3. **Anonymized Public Data**: Publicly available data that has been further anonymized and ethically reviewed
4. **Synthetic/Simulated Data**: Generated data for testing and development purposes

### Data Purpose

Datasets are created and maintained exclusively for:
- Academic research advancement
- Ethical AI development
- Trauma-informed care improvement
- Mental health research
- Public benefit research initiatives

## Third-Party Data Sharing

### We DO Share Data With:

**Approved Researchers** (with explicit agreements):
- Academic researchers at accredited institutions
- Non-profit research organizations
- Commercial entities with approved use cases
- All parties bound by strict license agreements

**Service Providers** (limited, necessary access):
- Cloud hosting infrastructure (encrypted data only)
- Security monitoring services
- Technical support contractors (under NDA)

**Legal Requirements**:
- When required by law or valid legal process
- With notice to affected parties where legally permissible

### We DO NOT Share Data With:

- Marketing companies or advertisers
- Data brokers or aggregators
- Social media platforms
- Consumer profiling services
- Political campaigns or advocacy groups
- Insurance companies
- Employers or background check services
- Law enforcement (except as legally required)

## Participant Rights

### Original Research Participants

While our datasets are fully anonymized and re-identification is prohibited:

**Right to Information**: Original data collection informed consent includes disclosure of potential research use

**Right to Withdraw**: Processes in place with original data collectors for participant withdrawal requests

**Right to Deletion**: Requests honored through original data collection partnerships (subject to anonymization limits)

**Right to Complaint**: Contact privacy@hollowhouseinstitute.org with concerns

### Dataset Users

Researchers and licensees have:

**Right to Know**: Full documentation of data sources, collection methods, and limitations

**Right to Transparency**: Clear terms of service and usage restrictions

**Right to Support**: Technical and ethical guidance for responsible use

**Right to Appeal**: Fair review of access denials or license revocations

## Privacy Framework Compliance

### United States

**California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)**:
- We do not sell personal information
- Opt-out mechanisms in place at data collection
- Privacy policy available and updated regularly

**Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)**:
- Source data collected under HIPAA compliance where applicable
- All health information fully de-identified per Safe Harbor method
- Business Associate Agreements with relevant partners

### European Union

**General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)**:
- Data minimization principles applied
- Purpose limitation strictly enforced
- Anonymization performed to GDPR standards
- Legal basis: Research purposes with appropriate safeguards
- Data Protection Impact Assessments conducted

**Note**: Our anonymization practices aim to ensure data falls outside GDPR scope as it cannot identify individuals.

### International

We comply with:
- PIPEDA (Canada)
- Privacy Act 1988 (Australia)
- LGPD (Brazil)
- Applicable privacy laws in all operating jurisdictions

## Anonymization and De-identification

### Our Standards

All datasets undergo rigorous anonymization following our [Anonymization Protocol](./anonymization_protocol.md):

1. **Direct Identifiers Removed**: Names, addresses, contact information, ID numbers
2. **Quasi-Identifiers Processed**: Dates generalized, locations aggregated, rare characteristics suppressed
3. **K-Anonymity Applied**: Minimum group sizes maintained
4. **Differential Privacy**: Statistical noise added where appropriate
5. **Expert Review**: Privacy professionals review all releases

### Re-identification Prohibition

All license agreements strictly prohibit:
- Attempts to re-identify participants
- Linking datasets to identify individuals
- Using external data sources for identification
- Reverse engineering anonymization methods

Violations result in immediate license termination and legal action.

## Data Retention and Deletion

### Our Practices

**Research Datasets**:
- Retained indefinitely for reproducibility and long-term research value
- Continuous security monitoring and updates
- Regular review of anonymization adequacy

**User Data** (access credentials, support tickets, etc.):
- Retained for duration of relationship
- Deleted within 90 days of termination
- Backup systems: Complete deletion within 180 days

**Application Materials**:
- Retained for 1 year
- Denied applications: Deleted after 6 months
- Archived for regulatory compliance where required

### User-Initiated Deletion

Licensees may request deletion of their user data:
- Email: privacy@hollowhouseinstitute.org
- Processing time: 30 days
- Confirmation provided upon completion

## Security Measures

We implement comprehensive security:

**Technical Measures**:
- Encryption at rest and in transit (AES-256, TLS 1.3)
- Multi-factor authentication required
- Access logging and monitoring
- Regular security audits and penetration testing
- Intrusion detection systems

**Organizational Measures**:
- Security awareness training
- Incident response procedures
- Background checks for staff with data access
- Principle of least privilege
- Regular security reviews

**Physical Security**:
- Data centers with SOC 2 Type II certification
- 24/7 monitoring and controlled access
- Redundancy and disaster recovery

## Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach:

**Our Response**:
1. Contain and investigate incident
2. Assess scope and impact
3. Notify affected parties within 72 hours (GDPR) or as required by law
4. Provide incident details and remediation steps
5. Cooperate with regulatory authorities
6. Implement corrective measures

**Notification Channels**:
- Email to all affected licensees
- Public disclosure if broad impact
- Regulatory notification as required

**Licensee Obligations**:
- Report suspected breaches within 24 hours
- Cooperate with investigation
- Implement recommended remediation

## Children's Privacy

The Hollow House Institute:
- Does not knowingly collect data from children under 13
- Requires age verification for dataset access
- Prohibits use of datasets to target children
- Requires parental consent for any child-focused research
- Follows COPPA requirements in the US

Datasets may contain historical data about minors:
- Collected with guardian consent
- Fully anonymized
- Subject to enhanced protection measures
