# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open, welcoming, and trauma-informed environment, we as contributors and maintainers of Hollow House Institute pledge to make participation in our project and community a respectful, safe, and harassment-free experience for everyone.

We are committed to:
- **Nervous-system safety**: Recognizing the embodied, relational nature of all interactions
- **Respect for boundaries**: Honoring personal and professional limits
- **Non-exploitation**: Ensuring research and collaboration are conducted ethically
- **Inclusive practices**: Welcoming diverse perspectives and experiences
- **Accountability**: Taking responsibility for our actions and their impact

## Our Standards

### Positive Behaviors

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy and compassion toward others
- Recognizing the emotional and relational dimensions of collaboration
- Honoring the foundational work and sovereignty of Amy Pierce Bui
- Maintaining appropriate boundaries in professional interactions
- Supporting trauma-informed communication practices

### Unacceptable Behaviors

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Aggressive, manipulative, or coercive communication
- Exploitative use of research, datasets, or frameworks
- Disregard for licensing terms (CC BY-NC-SA 4.0)
- Unauthorized commercial use of project materials
- Any conduct that creates an unsafe or dysregulating environment
- Deliberate misrepresentation of project principles or methodologies

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct, or to ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for behaviors they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including:
- GitHub repositories and issues
- Pull requests and code reviews
- Project documentation
- Community forums and discussions
- Social media interactions related to the project
- Any other communication channels associated with Hollow House Institute

It also applies when an individual is representing the project or community in public spaces.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by:
- Opening a confidential issue on GitHub (if safe to do so)
- Contacting the project maintainer directly through GitHub

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. We are committed to maintaining confidentiality regarding the reporter of an incident.

### Enforcement Guidelines

Project maintainers will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining consequences for any action deemed in violation of this Code of Conduct:

#### 1. Correction
**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or unprofessional behavior.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning with clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

#### 2. Warning
**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved for a specified period. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces and external channels. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

#### 3. Temporary Ban
**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period. No public or private interaction with the people involved is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

#### 4. Permanent Ban
**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment, or aggression toward individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any public interaction within the community.

## Trauma-Informed Principles

As a project rooted in nervous-system safety and relational psychology, we emphasize:

### Safety First
- We prioritize creating an environment where all participants feel safe
- We acknowledge that everyone has different triggers and needs
- We encourage clear communication about boundaries

### Regulation Over Reaction
- We practice pausing before responding when dysregulated
- We assume good intent until proven otherwise
- We recognize that conflict can be an opportunity for growth

### Relational Repair
- We believe in the possibility of repair after rupture
- We encourage accountability and genuine apology
- We support learning from mistakes

### Sovereignty and Respect
- We honor the sovereignty of the project's founder, Amy Pierce Bui
- We respect the intellectual and emotional labor embedded in this work
- We acknowledge the ancestral and embodied roots of these frameworks
